\begin{abstract}
Decentralised finance (DeFi) has become a densely interconnected financial system in which credit exposures propagate through tokenised liabilities, making timely \emph{risk monitoring} and \emph{decision recommendation} essential for investors, protocol operators, and regulators. Existing agentic pipelines for DeFi risk rely largely on static heuristics or general-purpose LLM reasoning, and lack a forecasting backbone for graph-structured exposure dynamics. We propose a forecast-grounded regulatory decision framework: forecast future exposure graphs with DeXposure-FM, measure network risk and scenario stress deterministically, translate the evidence into supervisory tickets, and gate intervention actions by data health and confidence. To evaluate this framework class, we introduce \emph{DeXposure-Bench}, a six-axis benchmark for FM\,+\,LLM decision pipelines on temporal exposure graphs, with regulator-aligned absolute-loss ground truth and eight reference method implementations. We instantiate the framework as \emph{DeXposure-Claw}, a deployable agentic system with ticket-level audit logs. On the frozen 2025 decision split, DeXposure-Claw raises ticket F1 from $0.0076$ (persistence\,+\,rules) to $0.0233$ (\textbf{$+208\%$}) and LLM-as-judge explanation quality from $2.03$ to $2.69/5$ (\textbf{$+33\%$}). The improvement is not free: FM\,+\,LLM variants improve recall and explanations at a non-trivial false-intervention cost ($\mathrm{FIR}\approx 0.44$) compared with rule-based baselines ($\mathrm{FIR}=0$). The benchmark therefore characterises DeXposure-Claw as a deployable Pareto option rather than a uniformly dominant replacement for conservative rules. We release the harness, dataset, reference implementations, and ticket-level audit logs to support extension.

\end{abstract}

\section{Conclusion}

We presented a forecast-grounded regulatory decision framework for DeFi exposure networks, the benchmark needed to test it, and a deployable agentic instantiation. The framework casts supervision as a forecast--measure--decide--gate pipeline: predicted exposure graphs feed deterministic monitoring and scenario engines, decision policies turn evidence into supervisory tickets, and safety gates determine when intervention-level actions are allowed. \textsc{DeXposure-Bench} makes this framework testable through six evaluation axes, a regulator-aligned absolute-loss ground truth, and reference implementations covering persistence, EvolveGCN, rule engines, snapshot LLMs, and FM\,+\,LLM variants. \textsc{DeXposure-Claw} packages the same framework as an auditable agentic system.

The main empirical lesson is not that every learned component dominates every conservative baseline. Rather, DeXposure-Claw moves the operating point: it raises ticket F1 from $0.0076$ to $0.0233$ ($+208\%$) and explanation quality from $2.03$ to $2.69/5$ ($+33\%$), while the benchmark makes the associated false-intervention cost visible. This is the role of the benchmark: it shows where the FM helps (trend, calibration, robustness), where persistence remains strong, where LLM decision improves recall and explanation, and where gates are required to keep deployment auditable. In this sense, the system is best read as a recall--explanation Pareto option for supervisory settings, not as a universal replacement for conservative rules.

\paragraph{Deployability characterisation}
For deployment, the important result is that the operating trade-offs are measured rather than hidden. The FM\,+\,monitor\,+\,scenario pipeline runs within a weekly supervisory cadence on a single RTX~4090, and every emitted ticket carries the prediction, monitor scores, scenario evidence, and, when applicable, LLM rationale that produced it. The ablations identify which components are load-bearing in clean operation and which act as reserves under degraded data or crisis dynamics. We release these ticket-level audit logs with the harness so that downstream operators can repeat the same characterisation under their own data conditions.

Beyond the limitations in \S\ref{sec:limitations}, two directions are most pressing. First, supervised use of the bench as a reward signal to fine-tune a regulator-specialised small model from the LLM rationales we release, closing the loop between offline evaluation and on-policy deployment. Second, extending the harness to additional on-chain risk surfaces---NFT lending, perpetual derivatives, cross-chain bridges---so the FM\,+\,LLM\,+\,gate template can be benchmarked beyond DeFi exposure, alongside a controlled regulator-in-the-loop study at sub-weekly resolution. We release the harness, dataset, eight reference implementations, and the ticket-level audit logs to invite the community to do both.

\section{Per-regime A1 (data-health gate) isolation}
\label{app:a1_isolated}
Table~\ref{tab:a1_isolated} expands Panel~B of
Table~\ref{tab:ablation} into the per-regime audit underlying the
``$\mathrm{FIR}\in[0.27,0.60]$'' range reported in the main text.
The four regimes vary which degradation channel is active
(\texttt{topo} only, \texttt{feat} only, both, and a milder
\texttt{severe} combination) and the three rows per regime sweep the
data-health threshold $\tau_{\mathrm{data}}$, holding the confidence
gate disabled ($\tau_{\mathrm{conf}}{=}0$) so that A1 is the only
suppression mechanism. The structurally important pattern is that
\emph{strict} ($\tau_{\mathrm{data}}{=}0.85$) drives the safe-mode
fraction to $100\%$ in every regime and collapses FIR to $0$ by
suppressing intervention tickets, while \emph{off} produces FIR up to
$0.541$.
