\begin{abstract}
Decentralised finance (DeFi) has become a densely interconnected financial system in which credit exposures propagate through tokenised liabilities, making timely \emph{risk monitoring} and \emph{decision recommendation} essential for investors, protocol operators, and regulators. Existing agentic pipelines for DeFi risk lack a dedicated forecasting backbone for graph-structured time series, limiting their data efficiency, uncertainty calibration, and robustness. Building on DeXposure-FM, a graph time-series foundation model pre-trained on $43.7$ million exposure-graph entries, we present \emph{DeXposure-Claw}: a four-layer agentic architecture---FM prediction, deterministic monitor and scenario engines, LLM decision, and a data-health/confidence safety gate---that turns predicted exposure graphs into auditable supervisory tickets. We also introduce \emph{DeXposure-Bench}, a six-axis evaluation suite for FM\,+\,LLM decision pipelines on temporal exposure graphs, with a regulator-aligned absolute-loss ground truth and eight reference method implementations. On the frozen 2025 decision split, the full FM\,+\,LLM\,+\,gate stack raises end-to-end ticket F1 from $0.0076$ (persistence\,+\,rules) to $0.0233$ (\textbf{$+208\%$}) and LLM-as-judge explanation quality from $2.03$ to $2.69/5$ (\textbf{$+33\%$}). The forecasting layer does not uniformly dominate persistence on static point metrics; its value appears in directional signal, calibrated prediction intervals (coverage $0.913$ against a $0.90$ target, ECE $0.013$), and lower mean degradation under corruption ($0.113$ vs.\ $0.148$). In a separate historical early-warning event study, the shared weighted-degree monitor gives $4$--$5$~week lead times on Terra/Luna, FTX, and SVB-USDC. These gains are a trade-off rather than uniform dominance: FM\,+\,LLM variants improve recall and explanations at a non-trivial false-intervention cost ($\mathrm{FIR}\approx 0.44$) compared with rule-based baselines ($\mathrm{FIR}=0$). We release the harness, dataset, reference implementations, and ticket-level audit logs to support extension.

\end{abstract}

\section{Conclusion}

We presented \textsc{DeXposure-Claw}, \textsc{DeXposure-Bench}, and an empirical characterisation of FM\,+\,LLM risk-decision pipelines on temporal DeFi exposure graphs. \textsc{DeXposure-Claw} turns predicted exposure graphs into auditable supervisory tickets through four layers: FM prediction, deterministic monitor and scenario engines, LLM decision, and a data-health/confidence safety gate. \textsc{DeXposure-Bench}, released alongside, provides a six-axis evaluation suite with eight reference method implementations and a regulator-aligned absolute-loss ground truth on the $4{,}300$-protocol \textsc{DeXposure} corpus.

The core empirical finding is that the full agentic stack improves the recall--explanation operating point while making its cost explicit. On the 2025 decision split, \textsc{DeXposure-Claw} raises end-to-end ticket F1 from $0.0076$ (persistence\,+\,rules) to $0.0233$ ($+208\%$), and LLM-as-judge explanation quality from $2.03$ to $2.69/5$ ($+33\%$). The forecasting layer is not a point-metric sweep over persistence: persistence remains stronger on PageRank MAE and static rank correlation, while the FM contributes trend signal, calibrated intervals (coverage $0.913$, ECE $0.013$), and lower mean degradation under corruption ($0.113$ vs.\ $0.148$). A separate historical event study shows that the shared weighted-degree monitor turns $4$--$5$ weeks before Terra/Luna, FTX, and SVB-USDC, but this is monitor/timeline evidence rather than a predictor-specific 2025 leaderboard result. The decision gain is a Pareto trade-off rather than uniform dominance: FM\,+\,LLM variants raise recall and explanation at a non-trivial false-intervention cost ($\mathrm{FIR}\approx 0.44$) compared with rule-based baselines ($\mathrm{FIR}=0$). Layer-wise attribution---$+32\%$ F1 from adding FM to a snapshot LLM, $+27\%$ from swapping rules for LLM under a fixed FM---shows that the predictor and decision layers are additive on recall-oriented F1; the safety gate then trades $3\%$ F1 for $+0.21$ judge score and $+10\%$ consistency, defining the auditability operating point.

\paragraph{Deployability characterisation}
Beyond aggregate metrics, \textsc{DeXposure-Bench} records the deployment properties that determine whether the system can be audited and operated. The FM\,+\,monitor\,+\,scenario pipeline (\texttt{m5\_fm\_rules}) processes one weekly snapshot in $67.6$~s on a single RTX~4090; the LLM decision layer adds an external API call whose latency is reported but not treated as a GPU benchmark. The ablations separate clean-data necessities from reserve mechanisms: removing the scenario engine collapses ticket precision to $0$, and disabling the confidence gate lifts FIR from $0$ to $0.429$ on clean 2025 data. Under $80$--$98\%$ feature/edge masking, the data-health gate becomes the fail-safe: without it, FIR rises to $0.29$--$0.60$; with strict safe-mode suppression, FIR returns to $0$. Multi-horizon monitoring is best read as a crisis-window coverage reserve, expanding alert counts by roughly $4\times$ during Terra/Luna, FTX, and SVB-USDC windows while preserving the same precision range. Every ticket carries the FM prediction, monitor scores, scenario projections, and, when applicable, LLM rationale that produced it; we release these audit logs alongside the harness.

Beyond the limitations in \S\ref{sec:limitations}, two directions are most pressing. First, supervised use of the bench as a reward signal to fine-tune a regulator-specialised small model from the LLM rationales we release, closing the loop between offline evaluation and on-policy deployment. Second, extending the harness to additional on-chain risk surfaces---NFT lending, perpetual derivatives, cross-chain bridges---so the FM\,+\,LLM\,+\,gate template can be benchmarked beyond DeFi exposure, alongside a controlled regulator-in-the-loop study at sub-weekly resolution. We release the harness, dataset, eight reference implementations, and the ticket-level audit logs to invite the community to do both.

\section{Per-regime A1 (data-health gate) isolation}
\label{app:a1_isolated}
Table~\ref{tab:a1_isolated} expands Panel~B of
Table~\ref{tab:ablation} into the per-regime audit underlying the
``$\mathrm{FIR}\in[0.27,0.60]$'' range reported in the main text.
The four regimes vary which degradation channel is active
(\texttt{topo} only, \texttt{feat} only, both, and a milder
\texttt{severe} combination) and the three rows per regime sweep the
data-health threshold $\tau_{\mathrm{data}}$, holding the confidence
gate disabled ($\tau_{\mathrm{conf}}{=}0$) so that A1 is the only
suppression mechanism. The structurally important pattern is that
\emph{strict} ($\tau_{\mathrm{data}}{=}0.85$) drives the safe-mode
fraction to $100\%$ in every regime and collapses FIR to $0$ by
suppressing intervention tickets, while \emph{off} produces FIR up to
$0.541$.
