\begin{abstract}
Decentralised finance (DeFi) has become a densely interconnected financial system in which credit exposures propagate through tokenised liabilities across protocols, making timely \emph{risk monitoring} and \emph{decision recommendation} essential for investors, protocol operators, and regulators. While recent “agentic” pipelines promise automated surveillance and intervention planning, this direction remains premature in practice, in part because of the lack of a reliable \emph{foundation model backbone} that can forecast the evolving exposure network with calibrated uncertainty and sufficient data efficiency. Building on DeXposure-FM, a recent graph time-series foundation model trained on over 43.7 million data entries, we develop \emph{DeXposure-Agent}, to our best knowledge the first agentic system that integrates a graph time-series foundation model backbone for DeFi exposure-network risk monitoring and decision recommendation. The system continuously ingests exposure snapshots, employs DeXposure-FM to predict future credit-exposure graphs, computes systemic risk indicators and standardised stress scenarios, and emits auditable alerts and conservative playbook-based recommendations under explicit data-health and uncertainty gates. We evaluate DeXposure-Agent across six benchmarks and stress-test settings, including low-data and weakly supervised regimes, and show that it (1) produces earlier and more stable early-warning signals of systemic fragility, (2) improves risk metric forecasting and trend detection beyond na\”ive baselines, and (3) yields higher-quality recommendation tickets with improved grounding and consistency when combining the foundation model backbone with LLM-based reasoning.

\end{abstract}

\section{Conclusion}

DeFi has grown into a tightly coupled financial ecosystem where tokenised liabilities link protocols into a dynamic web of credit exposures, so fast and reliable \emph{risk monitoring} and \emph{decision recommendation} are increasingly critical for investors, protocol teams, and regulators. Although recent ``agentic’’ workflows aim to automate surveillance and intervention planning, they are often not yet dependable in real deployments, largely because they lack a trustworthy \emph{foundation model backbone} capable of forecasting the evolving exposure network with strong data efficiency and well-calibrated uncertainty. Leveraging {DeXposure-FM}, a graph time-series foundation model trained on more than $43.7$ million data entries, we introduce \emph{DeXposure-Agent}, which to the best of our knowledge is the first agentic system that integrates a graph time-series foundation model backbone for DeFi exposure-network risk monitoring and decision recommendation. DeXposure-Agent continuously ingests exposure snapshots, uses {DeXposure-FM} to predict future credit-exposure graphs, derives systemic risk indicators and standardised stress-test scenarios, and produces auditable alerts together with conservative, playbook-driven recommendations governed by explicit data-health and uncertainty gates. Across six benchmarks and stress-test settings, including low-data and weakly supervised regimes, DeXposure-Agent (1) delivers earlier and more stable early-warning signals of systemic fragility, (2) improves risk-metric forecasting and trend detection beyond na\"ive baselines, and (3) generates higher-quality recommendation tickets with improved grounding and consistency when combining the foundation model backbone with LLM-based reasoning.
