\begin{abstract}
General-purpose LLM agents asked to recommend financial-risk
interventions can \emph{hallucinate severity}: handed evidence of
elevated risk, they over-read it, recommending high-stakes actions
whose every cited number is traceable to the input yet whose targets
are never actually stressed. We design and characterise a
\emph{deployment-ready} LLM-agent pipeline for decentralised-finance
supervision that measures and contains this failure mode: a graph
time-series forecaster produces predicted exposure networks,
deterministic monitors and stress scenarios convert them into a
typed evidence bundle, and the LLM emits supervisory tickets with
rationales. Two safety gates---a data-health gate and a confidence
gate---determine when intervention-level tickets are allowed; every
emitted ticket carries the evidence bundle and rationale that
produced it. We release an evaluation harness that scores such
pipelines on six axes, anchored to a \emph{regulator-aligned
absolute-loss} ground truth that replaces the fractional-change
metric used in prior systemic-risk evaluations, and additionally
measures LLM-as-judge explanation quality and a false-intervention
rate (FIR). With eight reference implementations and five years of
weekly DeFi exposure graphs, the full LLM-agent stack raises ticket
F1 from $0.0076$ to $0.0233$ ($+207\%$) at a visible
false-intervention cost (FIR~$\approx$~$0.44$ under an Opus~4.7
decision LLM vs $0$ for rules). A tier-above Opus~4.8 judge and a
cross-family panel (Gemini~2.5~Pro, GPT-5.5) all rank the full stack
top on explanation quality. Replacing the decision LLM with a
tier-below Sonnet~4.6 yields a Pareto improvement over
Opus~4.7 on every LLM-bearing axis (F1 $0.0288$, FIR $0.000$,
judge $2.65$) at $\sim$5$\times$ lower inference cost. We
characterise the resulting system as a recall--explanation Pareto
option for supervisory settings, not a uniform replacement for
conservative rules, and release ticket-level audit logs from the
historical backtest.
\end{abstract}
