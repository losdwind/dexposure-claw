\begin{abstract}
Decentralised finance (DeFi) exposes supervisors to fast-moving,
networked credit risk: shocks propagate across protocols before
human operators can inspect raw on-chain data. LLM agents are a
natural response, but they are hard to trust with high-stakes
interventions---handed weak or stale evidence they can
\emph{over-read} it, and prior systemic-risk evaluations give no
regulator-aligned way to measure the resulting false alarms. We
introduce \sysname{}, a forecast-grounded agentic supervision
system that routes LLM decisions through structured evidence
rather than raw transactions. \fmname{}, a graph time-series
foundation model, forecasts future exposure networks;
deterministic monitors and stress scenarios convert the forecasts
into typed alerts and scenario losses; data-health and confidence
gates decide when intervention-level tickets are admissible; and
every released ticket carries its evidence bundle, rationale, and
gate states for audit. On \benchname{}, a six-axis harness with a
regulator-aligned absolute-loss ground truth, five years of weekly
DeFi exposure graphs, and eight reference implementations, the
full stack lifts ticket F1 over a conservative rules baseline from
$0.0076$ to $0.0233$ ($p\!<\!10^{-4}$), at a visible
false-intervention cost under an Opus~4.7 decision model
(FIR~$\approx$~$0.44$). Swapping in a tier-below
Sonnet~4.6 raises F1 to $0.0288$, eliminates measured false
interventions, and directionally improves explanation quality at
$\sim$5$\times$ lower inference cost. These results position
\sysname{} as an auditable recall--explanation Pareto option for
human-in-the-loop DeFi supervision, not a replacement for
conservative rules; we release the evaluation harness and
ticket-level audit logs from the historical backtest.
\end{abstract}
