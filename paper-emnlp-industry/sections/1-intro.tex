\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Decentralised finance (DeFi) creates fast-moving networks of
token-mediated credit exposure among lending protocols,
decentralised exchanges, stablecoins, bridges, and yield
aggregators. The May~2022 Terra/Luna collapse erased over \$40
billion in 48~hours, and FTX and SVB/USDC followed within
months~\citep{VidalTomasEtAl2023FTX}: shocks traverse the network
faster than supervisors can inspect raw on-chain data, motivating
decision-support systems that forecast emerging exposure risk and
recommend timely supervisory responses.

A straightforward response is an LLM agent over on-chain
measurements. Our reference implementations (\S\ref{sec:results})
show the obvious designs fail in two ways. Reading
raw on-chain snapshots, the LLM is conservative but weak: poorly
targeted tickets (F1 $0.0183$) and the lowest-scored rationales
under every judge we test. Fed richer \emph{forecasted} evidence,
it targets better but \emph{hallucinates severity}: every cited
number is traceable to the evidence bundle (grounding $1.000$),
yet $44.8\%$ of its intervention-level tickets point at protocols
that are never actually stressed. The second failure is far
costlier in a supervisory setting: every intervention is
observable and politically expensive.

We introduce \sysname{} (\S\ref{sec:pipeline}), a
forecast-grounded agentic system for DeFi risk monitoring and
decision recommendation that routes LLM decisions through
structured evidence and \emph{measures} the over-intervention
cost that richer evidence induces. A graph time-series foundation
model (\fmname) forecasts future exposure networks; deterministic
monitors and stress scenarios convert the forecasts into typed
evidence; an LLM emits ranked supervisory tickets with targets,
severities, and evidence-linked rationales; and data-health and
confidence gates restrict intervention-level recommendations.
Every ticket is released with its evidence bundle, rationale, and
gate states for auditability.

We evaluate with \benchname{} (\S\ref{sec:bench}), a six-axis
harness over eight reference implementations. Its decision
axis scores tickets against a regulator-aligned
\emph{absolute-loss} ground truth (\S\ref{sec:preliminaries})
rather than the fractional-change ranking of prior systemic-risk
evaluations~\citep{BERTOMEU2024105321,gonon2025computingsystemicriskmeasures,
li2025hgmae}, so false interventions are measured directly---a
cost that finance-specific LLM-agent
work~\citep{yao2025arbiter,mao2025knowyourintent,luo2025llmcrypto,
jeon2026blindtrade} has not reported. Across five years of weekly
DeFi exposure graphs the full stack lifts ticket F1 from $0.0076$
(conservative persistence-rules baseline) to $0.0233$
($p\!<\!10^{-4}$), and three frontier judges across model
families~\citep{Zheng2023LLMJudge} all rank it highest on
explanation quality---at a visible false-intervention cost under
the Opus~4.7 decision LLM (FIR $\approx 0.44$). A tier-below
Sonnet~4.6 decision LLM gives the headline operating point: F1
$0.0288$, measured false interventions zero, explanation quality
directionally higher, at $\sim$5$\times$ lower inference cost.
\sysname{} is thus an auditable recall--explanation Pareto option
for human-in-the-loop supervision, not a replacement for
conservative rules.

\noindent
\textbf{Honest scope.} All results come from a historical
backtest over $283$ weekly snapshots of real DefiLlama-sourced
exposure data (2020--2025), not a live deployment---a
\emph{methods for deployed systems} and \emph{real-world
insights} contribution rather than a deployment case study; the
released ticket-level audit logs come from this backtest.
