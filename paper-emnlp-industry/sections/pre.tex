\section{Preliminaries}
\label{sec:preliminaries}

\paragraph{Exposure networks.}
We model DeFi credit exposure as a temporal weighted directed
graph. At week $t$ the exposure network is $G_t=(V_t,E_t,W_t)$,
where $V_t$ is the active protocol set, $E_t\subseteq V_t\times
V_t$ the directed exposure set, and $W_t[p,q]=w_{pq,t}\geq 0$ the
USD exposure of protocol $p$ to protocol $q$ (token-mediated
construction in \S\ref{app:fm-notation}). The incident exposure
mass of protocol $v$ is
$w_t(v)=\sum_{u}w_{uv,t}+\sum_{u}w_{vu,t}$; the observed history
$\mathcal{G}_{1:T}=\{G_1,\ldots,G_T\}$ carries node covariates
$X_t$.

\paragraph{Monitors and stress scenarios.}
At decision epoch $t$, a probabilistic temporal-graph forecaster
outputs $\mathcal{P}_{t,h}=P(G_{t+h}\mid G_{\leq t},X_{\leq t})$
for horizons $h\in\mathcal{H}$, summarised by a representative
predicted graph $\widehat{G}_{t+h}$ and Monte Carlo samples
$\widetilde{G}_{t+h}^{(m)}\sim\mathcal{P}_{t,h}$
(\S\ref{ssec:forecaster}, \S\ref{app:fm-notation}). A monitor is
a functional $\Phi_j:\mathcal{G}\to\mathbb{R}$ of a graph (e.g.\
top-1 PageRank, HHI concentration, density); an alert fires when
the forecasted statistic $\Phi_j(\widehat{G}_{t+h})$ deviates
from the mean $\mu_{j,t}$ of an $L$-week rolling baseline by
more than $z$ baseline standard deviations $\sigma_{j,t}$. A stress scenario is a graph perturbation
$s:\mathcal{G}\to\mathcal{G}$ with relative loss
$L(s;G)=1-\operatorname{Mass}(G^s)/\operatorname{Mass}(G)$,
$\operatorname{Mass}(G)=\sum_{e\in E(G)}w_e$; tail risk over the
Monte Carlo samples is the worst-$\lambda$-fraction mean
$\operatorname{CVaR}_{\lambda}(s,h)$
(\S\ref{app:pipeline-math}).

\paragraph{Supervisory tickets and ground truth.}
Each week the system emits ranked tickets $\tau=(u,Y,r)$: an
action $u$ from the four-action playbook
$\mathcal{U}=\{$\textsc{Monitor}, \textsc{Investigate},
\textsc{RecommendReduce}, \textsc{Contingency}$\}$, a target set
$Y\subseteq V_t$, and a rationale $r$. Ground truth is
regulator-aligned \emph{absolute} loss: with
$\Delta_t^h(v)=w_t(v)-w_{t+h}(v)$, the stressed set
$\mathcal{S}_t^h$ holds the top-$\pi$ fraction of protocols
ranked by $\Delta_t^h(v)$ among
$\{v:w_t(v)>0,\;\Delta_t^h(v)>0\}$. Ticket precision, recall,
and F1 score the union of ticket targets $\widehat{S}_t$ against
$\mathcal{S}_t^h$. With $\widehat{S}^{\mathrm{int}}_t$ the
targets of \emph{intervention-level} actions
(\textsc{RecommendReduce}, \textsc{Contingency}), the
false-intervention rate
$\mathrm{FIR}=\bigl|\widehat{S}^{\mathrm{int}}_t\setminus
\mathcal{S}_t^h\bigr|/\bigl|\widehat{S}^{\mathrm{int}}_t\bigr|$
is the fraction of intervention targets that are never actually
stressed.

\paragraph{Gates.}
A data-health score $DH_t\in[0,1]$ averages four data-quality
sub-scores; each alert carries a confidence $C_t(a)\in[0,1]$
that decays with low data health, wide Monte Carlo dispersion,
and longer horizons. Intervention-level actions require
$DH_t\geq\tau_{\mathrm{data}}$ and mean active-alert confidence
$\bar{C}_t\geq\tau_{\mathrm{conf}}$ (\S\ref{app:pipeline-math}).
