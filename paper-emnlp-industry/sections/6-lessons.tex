\section{Deployment Lessons}
\label{sec:lessons}

Three lessons should transfer to other forecast$\to$LLM pipelines
for high-stakes structured-evidence decisions.

\paragraph{L1. Grounding $\ne$ correctness.}
Every LLM variant we test achieves grounding $1.000$: every cited
number in every ticket traces to a field of the input evidence
bundle. The FM-grounded variants still pay
$\mathrm{FIR}\!=\!0.448/0.437$ (ungated/gated) under Opus~4.7. The
failure is not invented evidence but \emph{over-reading} of
weak-but-present evidence as justification for high-severity
action. Grounding says the agent did not hallucinate
citations; only a downstream intervention-realism metric (FIR
against an absolute-loss ground truth) says whether it is safe to
act on.

\paragraph{L2. Audit logs are a design artefact, not an output.}
Every ticket carries the forecast distribution, monitor scores,
scenario CVaRs, LLM rationale, gate states, and seeds that
produced it. We release these logs because our operating point is
not what every operator will need: a more conservative supervisor
can retune $\tau_{\mathrm{data}}$ or $\tau_{\mathrm{conf}}$ and
rerun the decision policy post-hoc, without re-invoking the FM or
the LLM.

\paragraph{L3. Safety gates are reserves, not always-on filters.}
On clean 2025 data the data-health gate is dormant; a leaderboard
without a degradation track cannot see it. Under masking it
suppresses up to $60\%$ of FIR-eligible tickets at the cost of
suspending interventions---so evaluate gates on dedicated stress
regimes, not only clean splits.
