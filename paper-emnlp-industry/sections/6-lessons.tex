\section{Deployment Lessons}
\label{sec:lessons}

Three lessons transfer to other forecast$\to$LLM pipelines.

\paragraph{L1. Grounding $\ne$ correctness.}
Every LLM variant we test achieves grounding $1.000$: every
cited number traces to a field of the evidence bundle. The
FM-grounded variants still pay
$\mathrm{FIR}\!=\!0.448/0.437$ (ungated/gated) under Opus~4.7. The
failure is not invented evidence but \emph{over-reading} of
weak-but-present evidence as justification for high-severity
action. Grounding certifies citations, not safety; only a
downstream intervention-realism metric such as FIR does.

\paragraph{L2. Audit logs are a design artefact, not an output.}
Every ticket carries the forecast distribution, monitor scores,
scenario CVaRs, rationale, gate states, and seeds that produced
it, so a more conservative supervisor can retune
$\tau_{\mathrm{data}}$ or $\tau_{\mathrm{conf}}$ and rerun the
decision policy post-hoc, without re-invoking the FM or the LLM.

\paragraph{L3. Safety gates are reserves, not always-on filters.}
On clean 2025 data the data-health gate is dormant---invisible to
a leaderboard without a degradation track---but under masking it
suppresses up to $60\%$ of FIR-eligible tickets: evaluate gates on
dedicated stress regimes.
