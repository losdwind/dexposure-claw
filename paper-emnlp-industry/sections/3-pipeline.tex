\label{sec:pipeline}

\sysname{} is a four-layer pipeline (Figure~\ref{fig:system}).
Layer~1 forecasts the near-future exposure graph with \fmname;
Layer~2 converts the forecast into a typed evidence bundle of
monitor alerts and stress scenarios; Layer~3 hands the bundle to an
LLM that emits supervisory tickets with rationale; Layer~4 gates
intervention-level actions on a data-health and a confidence check.
The pipeline runs at weekly cadence on a single RTX~4090; a
decision epoch takes under a minute of GPU time and well under a
dollar of LLM API spend (\S\ref{sec:bench}).

\subsection{Forecaster interface}
\label{ssec:forecaster}
At each decision epoch $t$ the agent observes a credit-exposure
snapshot $G_t=(V,E_t,W_t)$ (an inter-protocol token-mediated exposure
graph; see \S\ref{app:fm-notation}) and node covariates $X_t$. It
queries \fmname{} for a forecast distribution
$\mathcal{P}_{t,h}\!\leftarrow\!\text{\fmname.Forecast}(G_t,X_t,h)$
at horizons $\mathcal{H}\!=\!\{1,4,8,12\}$ weeks, constructs an
expected-weight predicted graph $\widehat{G}_{t+h}$ by thresholding
edge-existence probabilities at $\pi_{\min}\!=\!0.2$, and draws $M\!=\!50$
Monte Carlo samples for uncertainty. Full predicted-graph
construction equations are in \S\ref{app:fm-notation}.

\subsection{Structured evidence}
\label{ssec:evidence}
Layer~2 turns $\widehat{G}_{t+h}$ and the MC samples into a typed
evidence bundle. Five \emph{monitor functionals} score concentration,
fragility, and instability of the predicted graph: top-1 PageRank
\citep{BrinPage1998PageRank}, HHI concentration
\citep{Rhoades1993HHI}, network density, PageRank Gini, and degree
Gini. An alert fires when a monitor metric departs from its
$L\!=\!42$ week rolling baseline by more than $z\!=\!2$ standard
deviations; each alert is attached to (i) an attribution top-$K$
($K\!=\!5$) of contributing edges by marginal contribution, and
(ii) an uncertainty-aware confidence score $C_t(a)\!\in\![0,1]$
that decays with low data-health, wide MC dispersion, and longer
horizons.

In parallel, a \emph{scenario engine} applies five standardised
stress shocks---single protocol failure, bridge cluster failure,
stablecoin de-peg, sector lending shock, correlated top-$10$
stress---to both $\widehat{G}_{t+h}$ and each MC sample, and
aggregates the worst-half losses as
$\mathrm{CVaR}_{0.5}(s,h)$. Full
monitor and scenario definitions are in \S\ref{app:pipeline-math}.

\subsection{LLM decision layer}
\label{ssec:llm-decision}
Layer~3 receives the evidence bundle as a typed JSON payload---alerts
with attribution top-$K$, scenario CVaR table, MC dispersion, and the
scalar data-health score $DH_t$---and is asked to emit a ranked list
of supervisory tickets. Each ticket carries (a) a severity level
drawn from a fixed four-action playbook
(\texttt{Monitor}, \texttt{Investigate}, \texttt{Recommend-Reduce}, and
\texttt{Contingency}),
(b) a target set of protocols, and (c) a free-form rationale that
cites specific evidence fields by name. Self-consistency is probed
with three repeated calls at temperature~$0$ (Jaccard overlap on
targets and severities).

\subsection{Safety gates}
\label{ssec:gates}
Two gates determine the feasibility of intervention-level
actions. The \emph{data-health gate}
$DH_t\!=\!\mathrm{mean}(\mathrm{fresh}_t,\mathrm{miss}_t,
\mathrm{topo}_t,\mathrm{disc}_t)\!<\!\tau_{\mathrm{data}}\!=\!0.7$
triggers safe mode, in which only \texttt{Monitor} and
\texttt{Investigate} tickets are emitted, regardless of LLM
recommendation. The \emph{confidence gate}
$\bar{C}_t\!<\!\tau_{\mathrm{conf}}\!=\!0.6$ blocks
intervention-level tickets even when data is healthy. Every emitted
ticket carries the full evidence bundle, rationale, and gate
states; the audit log is the unit of release (\S\ref{sec:lessons}).
